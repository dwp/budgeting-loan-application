Text message (SMS)

General assumptions behind text messages:

  • users will see who the message is from through the sender name, which means there is less need to say this in the message itself
  • the URL to use will be a page on the current service domain, or at least similar in length
  • the application reference will be 6 characters long
  • the response code will be 4 characters long
  • we need to state the deadline for action for both the neutrally worded and offer messages, as any offer is valid for 14 days

Less than 160 characters including spaces

Text message drafts:

  • indicating approval - less than 160 characters
  • indicating rejection - less than 160 characters
  • neutrally worded - less than 160 characters

Underlying assumption: it will be better for users to receive the information in just one text message, to avoid any prospect of the message being split.

More than 160 but less than 320 characters including spaces

Text message drafts:

  • indicating approval - less than 320 characters
  • indicating rejection - less than 320 characters
  • neutrally worded - less than 320 characters

Underlying assumption: the vast majority of users will be able to receive a longer text message without it being split, and the greater length available will give them a clearer message that's easier to understand and act on
